hi, welcome, and thx for question

frequently apply variations of appreciative inquiry when facilitating
retros. the generalization for me is "positive" retros

for example, in the Agile Retro book there is the "locate strengths"
activity, a positive. the "strengths" can be things in the sprint, of
people in the team, etc. one scaling variation i use is: 1/2 of the retro,
go out and ask people in the building, "what is something you do or know
that wish others did/knew?" then in 1/2 half, collect the data, find themes
or vote for a winner, and then explore how to apply/do it in the next Sprint

another i call the "happiness retro." we randomly pick 1 person on the
team. then the rest interview this person for "what could we do that would
make you more happy in the next sprint?" and then the team decides on 1 or
2, creates a concrete plan for it, and then next sprint... executes to make
the person more happy ;)

when coaching, very frequentl focus on retros on the positive side with
variations of of appreciate inquiry, positives, etc
