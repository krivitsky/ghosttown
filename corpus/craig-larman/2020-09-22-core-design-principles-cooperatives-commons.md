picking up on james' question about this...

my 2019 LeSS conf talk was on the Econ Nobel prize work on groups that have
a "commons" (which perhaps includes cooperatives) and managing them
effectively: Lin Ostrum's Core Design Principles

in addition to their applicability to cooperatives, note that in LeSS there
are many "commons": code, shared responsibility for a done increment,
decisions/policies, and more

there's lots of online material, and here's a link to the post in this
group where i summarized them:
