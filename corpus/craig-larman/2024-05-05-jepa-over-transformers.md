transformers -- eg and esp decoder-only multi-layer feed-forward nn's --
have been the hot ml architecture since 2017, from google. my 2020 LeSS
conf keynote on ai & od highlighted the coming impact of transformers such
as gpt

yann lecun -- head of ai at meta, turing-award winner -- is an early,
influential, and deeply experienced ml researcher, widely considered one of
the best in the world.

lecun has pushed hard with his research teams (at meta & NYU) on
transformers for moving towards any hints of AGI or at least more general
learning... very much **without** success. BUT they have recently explored
"joint embedding predictive architecture" (JEPA), as a major architecture
to replace transformers, with interesting hints at success & progress

in 3 years with 1000 futures would bet in 100+ of those futures JEPA is a
dominant ml architecture... higher odds than other candidates
