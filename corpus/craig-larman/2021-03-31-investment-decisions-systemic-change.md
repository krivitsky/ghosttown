systems thinking 101: it requires a systemic change in *all* the elements of the org design working together. we emphasized that in the Org chapter of book 1. as the Galbraith Star Model -- and we -- emphasized, without a change in all the related OD -- structure, policy, people, tasks, rewards, etc -- it doesn't produce the result and falls apart

something u probably don't know is that i work with all the candidates on their case studies, plus because of my situation many many people over the decades share the details of their change attempts in orgs. in virtually every case, the repeating cause of the unsuccessful change is trying to "do some part of LeSS (or whatever intended deep change)" -- and it isn't about just LeSS. for any deep-change *org design framework* -- and LeSS is an org design FW -- it requires a systemic change of all the elements of the org design. BTW this is hardly a novel insight: if you study org change research, it's a repeating theme that "taking parts" falls apart.

that's why in book 3 one of the key adoption guides is *Parallel Org*, because it requires a wholistic change all together of structure, policy, people, tasks, rewards, processes, etc. that all-together OD change is possible when a parallel org (of e.g. 50 people to start) is used, in which it is as though a new company is being designed tabula rasa. but that doesn't work when trying to fit in to an existing org with "some parts"
