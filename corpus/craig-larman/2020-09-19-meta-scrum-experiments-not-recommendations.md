hi alexey, hope u r well, and thx for asking

first, widening my comment and perhaps speaking for both bas and me, we'd
like to emphasize that we chose the term *EXPERIMENTS* very deliberately.
these were not written as *recommendations*. now, that said, there is
surely some solid subset of the hundreds of experiments that even to this
day we heartily support and even *recommend*, but doubly so as a trainer we
want to ensure that what is understood and communicated about book 1 & 2 is
that these are *experiments*... some of them very context dependent, and a
few of them we'd probably today say "probably fails as an experiment.
probably don't try that one."

narrowing back in to your question: i don't recall that discussion, but can
still speak to it from my perspective:

i would suggest framing "meta-scrum for adoption" as an *experiment*! there
are some contexts in which it might work, and others not.

also and more specifically, having *just* an (1) impediment backlog, and
(2) management working in short impediment-service cycles with adaptive
iterative planning... *is not scrum*. let's change the language a little
but leave the point the same: *experiment*: managers have a list of
impediments and work on them, and adjust regularly based on learning.
that's a reasonable and common activity, but not the same as scrum. to
broaden out: i've seen the term "scrum" abused to mean just about anything,
as long as it vaguely involves a list and working in cycles -- and hey! ...
with stickies on wall! scrum! .... NOT. perhaps the old discussion was
making that point too

does that help? pls ask more if not
