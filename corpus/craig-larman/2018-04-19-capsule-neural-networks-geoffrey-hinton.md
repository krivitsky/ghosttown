the godfather of neural nets and deep learning, geoffrey hinton, recently published what he considers a milestone step forward, the capsule NN. it has so much potential over the prior state of the art, convolutional NNs -- also from hinton -- he recommends dropping the older NN models and starting afresh with capsule NNs


i.e. important


if you are working in code, you will increasingly bump into groups starting to apply deep learning NNs. met two today, applying to fraud detection, and fashion design and accessories


one key idea in capsule networks: rather than the current scalar inputs & output of a neuron, neuron capsules receive and send vectors, the canonical case being a vector of probability of observation, and associated pose (X, Y, Z + orientation vector). so what? orders of magnitude more information complexity can be modeled, leading to neuron capsules that represent higher-order objects than before, so that the capsule NN can model semantics and *multiple-perspective* relationships, not just "scenes". if you're familiar with the Gallilean Transformation from newtonian physics, this network of poses can be transformed and rotated to any alternate origin (0,0) so that multiple perspectives can be captured in the capsule NN.


hinton is the great-great-grandson of george boole
