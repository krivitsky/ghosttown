*if folks want to discuss other approaches (safe, "scrum 3.0", nexus,
person X's scaling framework, ...) or discuss "agile scaling framework"
critiques, and so forth, that's great. but please go do that in another
group*

why? wish to focus this group on LeSS topics specifically around
understanding and applying LeSS, with a great signal to noise ratio for
learning about LeSS adoptions, experiments, guides, and rules, especially
with an aim to help candidate trainers learn more about LeSS and help them
with their current LeSS adoptions

e.g., "what is this group's LeSS adoption advice in this context?", "what
RAs might be applicable in this context?", "should we start with an
all-at-once change for 4 or for 8 teams in this context?", "why do LeSS
adoptions avoid using any 'agile' management tools (including jira, rally,
version one, etc), and just use a simple cloud spreadsheet & wiki for the
PB?", "here's a summary of the current LeSS adoption am working in", "how
to approach the architecture of a large-scale c++ system and related build
system to support integrating continuously with shared code, in a LeSS
adoption?", "what is your experience in changing the roles, job titles &
career path definitions in an org and interacting with HR during a LeSS
adoption?", "here's our new idea for an org structure for this LeSS
adoption in an embedded domain with hw & sw dev; what do you think?", "if
forced to have line managers, should we set up a 50-1 ratio or a 100-1
ratio in this context?", "here's our story of eliminating projects/programs
in our LeSS adoption", "how did you approach safety critical constraints in
your LeSS adoption?"

and if you're a candidate LeSS trainer and thinking of "enhancing" scaling
with LeSS with your favorite add-on idea (multiple POs, BOs, "two track
scrum", program portfolio mgmt, prioritization method X, the UX group,
...), this is a great place to explore with all of us trainers here why
they are deliberately not part of LeSS ;)
