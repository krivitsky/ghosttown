on the adoption of all this: gene made an important point in this thread: it's not all-at-once for the entire company, it's for "50" people at the beginning of the new org model. so on "journey" and gradualistic comments... if the senior mgmt in a giant company can't fully commit to creating a new org with a completely new org design "top to bottom" for just 50 people (this is "deep & narrow" principle) in which there is indeed an all-at-once change in everything from investment practices to team structure etc, then there is something very suspicious about the claim by senior mgmt that they want to and are willing to actually make a deep change at some point

so if one can't get senior mgmt to agree to a serious deep change -- including investment decision making -- in a new org for just 50 people, as a pattern that's a sign that this group is unlikely to ever really deeply change in the future... unless and until they face an existential crisis

rather it will be a *loop journey* where the end point looks very much like the beginning, ala larman's laws
