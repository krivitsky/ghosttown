+1 viktor

regarding adoption, one of the LeSS *rules* -- not a future perfection goal -- is
*For the product group, establish the complete LeSS structure "at the start"; this is vital for a LeSS adoption.*
(where that group size starts at 50-ish people)

candidate trainers need to understand *why* that's a rule -- and that it is one, and then as future trainers reinforce this -- which is why have been spending time on this thread to share elaboration

it's basic systems thinking applied to org design. one example, shared above, is trying to create effective and sustained feature teams without also changing the job title, path, and reward system to be congruent. another example -- that kicked off this thread -- is that big-batch investment decisions by mgrs in the traditional way that are then pushed as projects of work into a sw product group (e.g. in my limited understanding but at least as seen in companies that visit, SAFe "epics->features->stories") are fundamentally inconsistent with the optimizing goal of LeSS -- sprint-by-sprint real adaptiveness decided by the independent PO, in the service of learning what to work on next of highest impact -- and it also generates the Contract Game and the "PO" as just a glorified proj mgr.

the point of the org design of LeSS is to actually create an org design that is consistent with global adaptiveness, and a set of inter-locking OD elements are required "at the start" to achieve that benefit

if some group wants to "do parts", then... whatever. but pls don't call it LeSS, or LeSS-like, or customized LeSS, etc. in most cases it will just devolve and play out larman's laws as another example of the endless treadmill of mgmt change fads in sw, fueling the change-fad industry and careerism

as viktor notes, distorting this as being "purist", "perfectionist", etc, quite misses the ("*it's a system*") point, and is a move/claim weaponized for decades to avoid actually making the OD changes that challenge the status quo. LL #3: *As a corollary to (1), any change initiative will be derided as "purist", "theoretical", "revolutionary", "religion", and "needing pragmatic customization for local concerns" — which deflects from addressing weaknesses and manager/specialist status quo*.

something worth repeating that gene brought up: if the senior mgrs of a giant company of 50K people can't commit to a full experiment of just 50 people in a parallel org that *establish the complete LeSS structure "at the start"*, the likelihood that they are actually serious about significant change to that "in the future" is probably just political change theatre, something many of us have seen many times
