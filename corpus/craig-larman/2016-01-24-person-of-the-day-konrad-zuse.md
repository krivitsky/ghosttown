less known outside germany (he's got a stamp!), zuse
invented & built the first modern and turing-complete computer in the late 1930s, the Z1 through Z4,
and in 1941 become the founder of the first computer company for the Z4. he
invented the first high-level programming language, plankalkul, which has
some similarities to APL (the language i started work as a professional
programmer). his work was not so well-publicized since he was funded by the
nazis, though IBM licensed his patents in 1946.

just warming up, in 1967 he wrote *Calculating Space*, which posited the
idea of digital physics and the universe as a computational information
system. this was picked up by john wheeler in 1970 and became quantum
information theory, and very recently became central to what is arguably
the most important new concept in physics in the last decade, from
maldacena and susskind, that ER = EPR, i.e., the einstein-rosen bridge at
the grand scale of spacetime ("wormhole") is *actually just another
description of the same phenomena* as quantum-level einstein, rosen, and
podolsky quantum entanglement ("spooky action at a distance"). i.e.,
spacetime is just a way to account for quantum information.

good work, konrad zuse!
