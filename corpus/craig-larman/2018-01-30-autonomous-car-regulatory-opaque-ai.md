thx for this. a seldom explained aspect in these stories is the regulatory
aspect, that varies from zone to zone. for example, in germany under
existing federal regulations, it is required that a fault in a device with
software can be (more or less) deterministically traced to some code,
assuming code is the causal area. this creates either limitations on the
degree of learning machines (e.g., deep learning ANNs) that can be used
(since how an ANN works is opaque), or on pushing the limits of
interpreting the regulations. add to this mix that in some auto companies,
individual people will be held "accountable" for faults, including legally
liable.

all this makes me wonder if the focus on usa partnering of europe makers
(e.g. VW) is partly driven by these forces, in addition to the
talent/experience force basically all stemming from google & the insanely
prolific sebastian thrun, who is ironically german ;)

longer term, with the rise of opaque learning machines, don't think the
"deterministic" regulations can stand, and predict there will be move
towards automated test & verification of black boxes as the criteria,
rather than understanding inside the box. ... i.e., it will be like working
with humans ;)
