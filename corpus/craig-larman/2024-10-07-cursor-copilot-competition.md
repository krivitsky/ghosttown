Cursor is emerging as leading next-gen ai-based dev tool ("editor")
experiencing rapid market share growth, largely by reduction in market
share for other tools (NB! versus MS VSCode copilot), and secondly with
influx of non-experts who can more easily start to dev with Cursor

(below will make biz & OD point related point to all this)

waaay back in aug 2024 (a lifetime ago re ai improvements) had 30K paying
customers, with a valuation of 400M, and no shortage of VC funding. and its
biz success & solutions are *accelerating*

first, it illustrates the *fool's errand* of evaluating & concluding about
any ai tool capability... in the Stone Age 4 months ago, some "Igor" could
have evaluated it and said "blah blah not bad, but copilot is better".
watch out for such mistakes! conclusions about any particular ai tool are
premature

second and more important, on to the biz & OD point:
an interesting Lex Fridman interview with the Cursor team just came out:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oFfVt3S51T4
(also available as a podcast on all platforms)

note at 14:35 forward, paraphrasing:
"lex: copilot is feeling stale and has slow release of new features; cursor
is fast becoming superior"

*14:50 NB "one thing that helps us that we're [a team of 4 multi-learning
devs] doing it ALL IN ONE. we're developing the UX, we're developing the ai
model... the same ppl are developing the entire solution end to end... the
person doing the ui and training the model are often the same person"*

the org structure of MS Copilot? don't know for sure, but searching job
titles & job openings at MS related to copilot for code hints at lots of
manager positions, and lots of single-specialist ppl and groups ala is
common at MS:
AI Model Development Team.
UI/UX Design Team
Backend Engineering Team
Product Management Team
Quality Assurance (QA) Team
Security and Compliance Team
Customer Support and Feedback Team

;) and the key point is that the adaptiveness and low lead time
optimizations that come from *4 multi-learning devs* is beating MS... in a
product market space with *huuuge* $$ opportunities and strategic
implications

the 4 (young) ppl *incorrectly* summarize their advantage as "a startup",
but that's not the correct advantage, cuz as they themselves explain, their
advantage is "... [4 multi-learning devs] doing it ALL IN ONE. we're
developing the UX, we're developing the ai model... the same ppl are
developing the entire solution end to end... the person doing the ui and
training the model are often the same person."

there is no legal or biz or OD reason that MS couldn't have organized the
same way, and there's a chance that their traditional mgmt emphasis on
single-specialists ppl & groups will lead to their decline in this hot new
market... doubly ironic as they are trying to make a tool that allows 1
person to learn & do more varied skills
