during 2021 spent time at United HealthCare (and Optum sub-company) with
senior mgmt, introducing ST, OD, and LeSS, with a focus on them owning the
insights thru a focus on them doing a lot of systems modeling

we often see change theatre rather than real deep change, so is nice to
share & remember stories of actual deep change, and more important, that it
makes an impact with respect to system goal (adaptiveness to discover
value) and biz objectives... more contracts/biz in this case, and more
customer/user delight

in this case the "optum serve" group: they create sw supporting medical
tests & evaluations related to insurance. only have this data 2nd hand but
to my limited insight: 400 ppl in a LeSS Huge adoption, vastly simplified
OD, moving to explicit "product developer" as key role with a focus on
multi-learning, shared code, lots of feature teams, elimination of most of
the traditional mgr roles/behaviors, a real PO & APOs

interestingly, they did it all-at-once (the default LeSS rule) with all
400. it was apparently chaotic for a few weeks, but it worked out. they did
self-designing teams. in contrast we normally suggest "50" ppl batch size
into the change, on a slower incremental path.

always enjoy hearing about groups that make real change and get a benefit
