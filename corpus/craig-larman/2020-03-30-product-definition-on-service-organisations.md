hi and thx for question

in LeSS we first focus on hw/sw products. while of course a
service-perspective is great, and systemic design that includes both
service and hw/sw products is great, and there's lots to do in improving
service and org design for it, that's not the first-step focus of a LeSS
adoption per se. rather, the first-step adoption focus is on a concrete
hw/sw product we are developing (developing code or a chip, by computer
scientists, etc), that of course may be (often is) an enabler to a broader
service.

for example, in a retail bank, the first-step adoption focus of the product
group adopting LeSS is the retail banking sw product. of course this
intersects with the bank's services, so no point in falling into a false
dichotomy about this. but we *start* with the product group (Prod Mgmt &
Dev) creating and using a hw/sw product, in the aim of delivering and
supporting services

suggest not to turn this topic into a false dichotomy discussion of "it is
unskillful to not consider service too." of course that's important,
especially from a systems thinking view

but perhaps most especially, the product in the first step of a LeSS
adoption is not just a service definition or a service-level def. it is a
hw/sw definition.

e.g. Our first sw product def for a LeSS adoption here at the power company
is... *a sw system that manages the power and most customer use cases
(onboarding, billing, ...)*.

something like "stable, high quality, ..." is service goal (and that's
great), but is not itself the sw product that would be the adoption focus
of a first step in a LeSS adoption.
