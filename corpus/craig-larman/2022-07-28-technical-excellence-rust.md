as shared before, large products often involve embedded or systems
programming, and traditionally used c or c++. both google and ms estimate
that something around 3/4 of their bugs (security, ...) are related to the
use of c or c++ (intrinsically unsafe languages).

i urge anyone involved in systems or safety-critical sw to asap start the
discussions moving from c/c++ to rust, in the interest of a safer and more
efficient world. i shudder at the knowledge there is c++ code in planes and
cars.

the march of rust continues to grow. linus himself is indicating rust will
be allowed as the linux kernel dev language (the first and only other than
C), which is simply huge for the future of rust & then for the better
future of sw & humanity
https://thenewstack.io/rust-in-the-linux-kernel-by-2023-linus-torvalds-predicts/

meta has now endorsed rust for the server side:
https://engineering.fb.com/2022/07/27/developer-tools/programming-languages-endorsed-for-server-side-use-at-meta/

and google has joined the rust foundation, as they too want it to succeed
as the successor to c & c++
https://opensource.googleblog.com/2021/02/google-joins-rust-foundation.html

note that rust is also used for WASM, indicated here some years ago, and
now is picking up steam as the important tech it promised
