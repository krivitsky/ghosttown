folks,

have noticed a few comments about "best practices". as the 2nd LeSS book
emphasizes in chapter 1, repeating the point made by don reinertsen and
mary poppendieck, there are no best practices in R&D and that paradigm is
flawed in the context of R&D and continuous improvement. pls read chapter 1
for the details, quotes, and context.

such basic messages coming from LeSS (e.g., ones emphasized in the open
chapters of the books) should be very clear for the trainers in their
communications with others.
