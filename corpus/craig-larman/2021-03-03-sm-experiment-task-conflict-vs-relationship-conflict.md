this is a distinction had learned before, but sorta forgot. in social psych
there is a growing body of evidence for the existence of task conflict vs
relationship conflict... not surprising, as not difficult to observe. RC is
kinda obvious; task conflict refers to "conflict" arising from activities
such as 2+ people involved in discussing an understanding of something,
making a decision, etc.

there is also growing research that a team/group that can
emotionally/psychologically separate TC from RC can fare better. e.g.
* if the people are considering a decision, and there are disagreements,
and the people understand in their bones that this is just disagreements
with the goal of successfully making a better decision in a TC framing, and
is not "personal".

* people are discussing some topic and they "challenge" each others facts,
assumptions, etc with the intent to get closer to better understanding, in
a TC framing

people that know and agree that they are in a TC framing won't let that
leak as much into RC. and of course OTOH people who don't know the
distinction or the current framing have trouble separating these and then
TC metastasizes into RC more frequently

i see connections of this in for example "the 5 dysfunctions of a team."
e.g. if a team avoids conflict it might be because they can't separate TC
from RC

in addition to the degree of insight/education/coaching that people have
about TC vs RC as making a difference, i speculate that some of the Big5
personality traits (perhaps Agreeableness or Neuroticism) might also impact
the degree that a person can learn to separate TC and RC

seems to me that coaching people and teams on understanding TC vs RC is
worthy experiment in the journey of a team
