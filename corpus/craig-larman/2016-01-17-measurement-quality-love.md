quote of the day:

*Avedis Donabedian, a professor at the University of Michigan's School of
Public Health, was a towering figure in the field of quality measurement.
He developed what is known as Donabedian's triad, which states that quality
can be measured by looking at outcomes (how the subjects fared), processes
(what was done) and structures (how the work was organized). In 2000,
shortly before he died, he was asked about his view of quality.*

*"The secret of quality is love," he said.*
