>
> I am working with a client who has 4000 developers
>

limit the first LeSS adoption to 1 "small" "50 person" group with lots of
coaching, and lots of time (6 months?) before even attempting a second
group adoption. resist and if possible prevent broader adoption. do
everything possible to make the one and only one adopting group a success
and create "street cred." if possible, choose a product that is of high
interest, or that has an upcoming investment initiative of high interest.
ship every sprint, from sprint 1, if at all possible. shipping speaks much
louder than words, and success sells broader adoption/change support.

create a "marketing plan" for how the group will market/sell the success of
that first (and then first, and second) adoption

btw, have come to see that pressure for broad/fast adoptions exist in
financial services domain because of mgmt bonuses and objectives to "do the
adoption." watch out for that, and prevent/eliminate it.

> and we have identified three roles that will play a part in the process,
> these are:
>
>
> 1. Trainers
> 2. Team coaches (both technical and process)
> 3. Organisational coaches
>

i separate tech coaches from team coaches (a good SM), because it's very
different skills. i also recommend a special Sprint 1 coach: someone
well-experienced in LeSS acting as master of ceremonies, to teach the group
during Sprint 1 some way to go from SP1 to Overall Retro, and everything in
between.

my list:

trainers (Scrum & LeSS)
tech coaches
team coaches (good SMs)
SMs coach
PO coach
mgmt coach
org coach
Sprint 1 coach

all this for the one and only one 50-person group, with lots of focus, for
months. nothing broader. of course, 1 person can play several coach roles

i also recommend *no* role-based training (for Scrum and LeSS). e.g., no
CSM vs CSPO training. no mgmt vs. other-role training. rather, all
together. i.e., everyone in a CLP course.
