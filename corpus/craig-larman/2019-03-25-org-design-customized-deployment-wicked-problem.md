agree completely

but there still remains the challenge of (eg) 20 simultaneous customization deployments, in terms of group structures and lists of work (backlogs). it's similar context to the zodiac case of inflight entertainment systems and the ion trading system case of in-bank customization that (picking up on tim's "first case" question in this thread) iirc drove me original questions (and one sees this in SAP deployments too ). that is, (eg 20) deployment teams may be physically at the (20) customer sites (all in parallel) for some months, each with their own work lists

and as mentioned in original post, in the variant org design solutions have seen for this ("deployment groups do customization", "central product group does customization", ...) each seems to be in a "wicked problem" space with some tough consequences. i.e., no neat solution

as a rule of thumb, like to keep probing for stories and experiences in wicked problem cases to get more data. in general, seems to me, these "trade-offs" cases usually are best handled by exploiting patterns, so am interested in getting stories of context, forces, etc, to discover context-specific patterns that can be exploited
