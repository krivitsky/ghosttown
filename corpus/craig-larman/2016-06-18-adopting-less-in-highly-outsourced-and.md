these are political survival ideas, not successful-change ideas, since it
seems to me the tech group is too unskillful to bother trying to help

without criticizing it per se, perhaps shift people away from all jargon,
including: agile, safe, less, scrum, kanban, stories, lean, etc ... and
encourage people to talk in "plain italian" about behavior or optimization.
e.g., "we want to work on highest customer value." avoid saying or writing
"agile", "safe", "less", "scrum", "lean" ;)

since it is a bank, it is likely that any change will eventually fail, or
be undone, due to politics/careerism/etc. therefore maybe it would be
strategically better to associate yourself as the "customer value" guy (or
some fad-and-jargon-free phrases that CEOs will always like. i.e.
"motherhood value statements"), not the "agile/etc" guy, so that when the
failure or backlash comes, you are more distanced. in general, distance
yourself from the change fad and jargon (without criticizing it), and if
possible, ASAP get a transfer to a position that is unrelated to change,
agile, etc.

if you are forced to participate in the "agile change planning", another
idea is to try to have someone else visibly associated with the plan, the
change, and its outcome, not you. if they are "doing safe", then try to
delegate or shift the plan as much as possible to a "safe person", who is
seen as the planner and associated with safe, not you. do not criticize any
of the existing fads or interests or jargon (or you will be blamed as the
cause of the change failure). and during all this, communicate "especially
interested in focusing on our customers and delivering customer value, over
particular processes"... and ASAP get a transfer to a position that is
unrelated to change, agile, etc., ... and if that can be more towards the
customer side (e.g. product mgmt in the bank) than "delivery" side, it
might be less risky, actually interesting, and also consistent with your
messaging.
