hi tim, and thx for bringing up "PDCA". that's the generalization of the
point of the experiment under discussion.

and PDCA/PDSA is not scrum; far too many people blithely conflate these as
equivalent. scrum has many more specific elements regarding org design.
that's why experiment was NOT summarized as or intending to describe scrum
or meta-scrum. it is just a PDSA cycle with a list

lean startup, scrum, design thinking, etc etc are not equal to PDSA; they
have some *intersection set* with PDSA, or could be viewed as
specializations of it, but contain many additional elements to be precisely
categorized as scrum, etc

btw, my old friend tom gilb (who know deming) shared with me the deming did
not like the old "PDCA" being attached to him per se. why? he developed
*PDSA* (PD*S*A) as an improvement over the older PDCA (ala shewhart), and
wanted people to understand why PDSA was different and more useful. there
must be online articles on this history somewhere
