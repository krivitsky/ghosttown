my step-1 test of willingness of engagement of senior mgmt is that before i
will come and work with them, *they have to all agree to complete a set of
about 400 pages of readings in preparations*. it's interesting that that
quickly weeds out a lot. as a short example story, many years ago with
Siemens Trains, after learning about the readings prep, the senior mgrs
replied to me, "we don't have time to learn this stuff, but we'll delegate
it to the project managers." ;) i have come to the view that there's very
little point in wasting time in such groups, if you care about using your
own time with some hope of making a lasting positive impact

OTOH some senior groups respond, "fantastic! when can you send the reading
list?" ... and then i know there's some chance of progress beyond *change
theatre* and mgmt careerism games.
