recently heard an interview with dan pink, who as y'all probably know is
interested in supporting intrinsic motivation at work, which dovetails with
"own, not rent" or "want to, not have to" suggested approach for LeSS
engagements. as a brief aside in the talk, he mentioned a technique he
regularly finds effective related to motivation, that he picked up from
"motivational interviewing" technique.

could be applied by scrum master, or e.g. between pairs in a team retro.


1. regarding some topic for which there is a lack of motivation or lack of
action/change, ask something like, "on a scale of 1 to 10, how would you
rate your intrinsic motivation for this issue?"


2. ask, "why isn't it *lower*?"
that's an interesting question


motivational interviewing (from my very limited intro) emphasizes no
judgement, and a focus on empathy and understanding. you can see that these
questions are pretty non-threatening, and lead to the person doing a
self-inquiry motivation exploration in a non-pushy way


simple technique. on my list to experiment with
