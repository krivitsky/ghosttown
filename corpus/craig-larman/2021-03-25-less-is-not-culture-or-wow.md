every couple of years, seems useful to reiterate this point here. as far as
can tell, the confusion is often related to experiences people have had in
coaching that were based on just locally changing ways of working,
"mindset", and so forth. and also related to coaches not directly working
with c-level mgmt that has the power to actually deeply change org design.
so then some incorrectly think of LeSS (like their prior local & minor
changes) as some kinda process change, WoW change, "culture change", etc.
and also this misunderstanding seems related to the myriad fake changes
that one sees where the labels and WoW were locally changed, but not the
groups/teams, policies, career paths, and other major org design elements.

one even sees this profound misunderstanding in the new PMI book, "choose
your WoW."

all that misses the point: LeSS is a *total organizational design change*,
primarily of *structural* design (groups, depts, teams, hierarchies, career
paths, mgmt, sites, etc) in the org to be consist with adaptiveness as the
global optimizing goal. and to broaden this point, LeSS is a change in OD
to be consistent with adaptiveness as the goal, covering all major elements
of OD: structure, policies, processes, reward systems, people, and so on.

the framing/title of the new PMI book (and its highlights) is an example of
what a deep confusion many folks have. large-scale org systems don't change
their first-order major dynamics if only WoW are changed. for example, LeSS
requires the elimination of projects/programs, PMO, project mgrs, etc --
all the elements of the Contract Game that are inconsistent with
adaptiveness. that is so very different (and far deeper and broader) than
"choosing your WoW." of course, by LL #1-4, all this becomes distorted, to
maintain the status quo for the traditional existing power structures &
positions.

and larmans law #5, *culture follows structure*, is meant to hint at a key
point: in large-scale org design: one can't directly change the broad
culture first if the structures (and other OD elements) are inconsistent.
it starts with changing structure and other OD elements -- eliminating
existing single-function teams, component teams, single-function roles &
career path, mgr positions, etc. in large scale, without changing the
system first, behavior/mindset/culture won't really change, and if it does
in some local area, that will be temporary and eventually revert to mean
