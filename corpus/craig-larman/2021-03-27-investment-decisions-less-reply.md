*"I understand in LeSS we are not focusing on that level."* maybe don't understand your point. in a LeSS adoption it's useful to first focus at the level that has the decision making authority to change the org design as described in the Org chapter of book 1, to be consistent with adaptiveness as the optimizing goal: eliminating traditional budgeting & investing models, projects, a PMO, existing HR policies, mgr roles, etc.

and in many companies that's the c-level executive group, and then common to start there

so if you mean in LeSS that we don't include focussing from the start the senior level, that's a misunderstanding. thus in the 3rd book the adoption principle "*top down and bottom up*", and in book 2 "inspect & adapt" (adoption) chapter there's the experiment *Try...Adoption with top-down management support*

not really interested in changing LeSS to fit into that status quo in companies "that have too much money" (to deeply change), but rather into companies where there is enough sense of urgency or existential crisis at the senior level to start by fixing the root cause problems in the org design, and thus eliminating from the start those traditional elements for the product group changing their OD to LeSS. i.e. actually changing the org to LeSS, rather than vice versa

so then from the start there would be the elimination of "there are people other than the APO or PO that have business responsibility to make large investment decisions." and then the original investment question goes away, and it's back to scrum as intended: the PO makes *all* the decisions, large & small. in contrast to "fake LeSS under layers of conventional mgmt." of course that usually only happens when a company has a true sense of urgency and the executive level is serious about giving up their old model. but since most companies have too much $$, what happens instead is... careerism- and fadism-driven change theatre, the Agile Industrial Complex, and so on, ala larman's laws

for gradualist change cases, pls see in book 3 the guides *Feature-Team Adoption Map*, and *Expanding Definition of Done*
