kotter (as most of u likely know) is a harvard biz school prof specializing
in org change research

recently ran across this quote
<https://digitaltransformation.net/en/interview/a-conversation-with-dr-john-kotter-about-change-leadership-in-a-fast-moving-world/>
from him that is relevant to how a group needs to own and self-design their
org change experiments, with less prescription and less packaged advice
from outside:

*"I know what I wouldn’t have done, and that is outsourcing the problem to
McKinsey… and have them find a solution. The research results are clear.
The solutions must come from within the organization."*

for some of u perhaps this quote (and the underlying research) can help
motivate a discussion with clients to own and design more themselves, with
only "barely sufficient" taken in from outside. NB: "barely sufficient"
depends on the "shu-ha-ri" state of the org, and LeSS is aiming towards
"barely sufficient" especially for a shu org... or at least that's how i
have viewed it
