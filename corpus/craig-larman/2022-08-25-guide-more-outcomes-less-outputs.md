recently listened to talk with ceo of duolingo. mentioned they have
"metrics-based" teams (eg retention, daily use), that reflects the LeSS
guide "more outcomes, less outputs".

in this approach some PB items are expressed as quantified goals, not
features. eg.
"shippers can find all top-quartile shipping options in less than 1
second."
"patient onboarding completes in less that 15 seconds, with less than 1
input error per 10,000"

and teams (end-to-end in this model) are independently responsible for
creatively exploring solutions, A/B testing to learn, etc

thus the PO is not generating so many features to implement, and is not
updating the PB so frequently, since more items are semi-stable until goal
achieved or replaced, and more decisions are delegated to the teams

this is an old and important approach esp promoted by tom gilb since the
70s, also in "impact mapping", etc

it is also associated with more job satisfaction as teams members are
creatively engaged, not micro-managed, are connected to real outcomes, and
own the results

OTOH no false dichotomies! true feature items do have a time and place,
when it would just be pedantic/awkward/wasteful to express things only as a
quantified outcome/goal rather than an output (feature) eg "single sign
on", "stop at red lights" are clear and necessary features, and trying to
recast them as just outcomes is not needed

we heartily encourage (and encourage candidates to encourage) "more
outcomes, less outputs" for its several benefits, and moving somewhat away
from just feature lists
