tim born asked about group-level decision making in LeSS. (beyond one team)...

note that there is no manager or specialist in LeSS that is allowed to decree most decisions (excepting legal things, corporate policies, and what not), so this is a common issue of self-managing at the product group level.

will share my 2 cents, but this is an area ripe for experimentation, and welcome all your observations/experiments/ideas

first, to frame this in terms of LeSS, this is a *coordination* topic. note that in book 3, there is a dedicated chapter

who may make the decisions for the entire group?
* everyone
* a community (with a caveat below)

by default, a community does not have the authority to make decisions for the group; only proposals. however, "everyone" can decide to delegate decision making to a community (and hence have the future power to take it away). e.g. iirc bmw self-driving car did that with arch comm, but can't recall for sure.

in both cases, my (this is not a LeSS guide, just my experiment) latest decision-making protocol suggestion for large groups is to take inspiration from *political chambers* processes (parliament, etc) models, with 3 phases: (1) proposals (2) discussion/pitches, (3) voting

== proposals
sometimes the topic is simple enough that written (or video) proposals are too much effort. if so, skip to "discussion"

otherwise, the proposal can be detailed in a digital artifact (or alternate ones for alternate proposals), with commenting capability. could be videos, not just text

as in political systems, the proposal or proposals should have been in place well before the upcoming discussion session, so that people have a chance to learn, contemplate, comment, etc. as in political systems, this may lead to evolution of the proposals before the formal discussion event

== discussion/pitches:
suggest, as in political chambers, that discussion (or as often the case in politics, a pitch speech) is at a clear time and place as an event (this can be with VC tools too), and with speaking rules, with a moderator. time limits, a chance to say one's piece without interruption, structured Q&A. etc.

the result may lead to further evolution of the proposals

== voting:
there are at least 2 situations: single proposal, alternate proposals. these require diff voting methods & wining policies.

if it is "everyone" voting (versus a community simply together in a room), suggest a digital tool

a guiding principle: weakly supported decisions are not a good idea in product groups. as such, suggest some kind of *supermajority* required to win. e.g. for single proposals, at least 2/3 "yes"

if a supermajority can't be achieved, time for Scrum Masters to engage with group with adaptation mechanisms, such as some variation of the "core protocols" "Resolution" protocol, etc. note that this too is a common political pattern.

==== the Bridgewater (ray dalio) method
if you aren't familiar with ray dalio & "radical transparency" and his book Principles, and the Bridgewater method of group decision making, recommended. have never tried this approach, but if you know it you know it has a number of compelling elements, including weighted votes in diff domains, reflecting one's expertise in a domain. and the results speak; Bridgewater has been extraordinarily successful for decades in their decision making.
