recently spent time at a large company with middle mgmt, so could inquire
some trends re remote work in their "agile" context. my key observation is
that the org has no awareness of the idea of people learning from each
other, or multilearning, and influence of "swarming" (xp term "whole team
together", scrum term "scrumming", similar to "mob working") to create
rapid cross-learning and multilearning. they have viewed and measured
"productivity" on individual single-specialists outputting the one thing
they currently know how to output, and didn't see much diff after going
remote (since they never had real teams swarming nor cross-learning &
multilearning).

from this guessed and observed that it was quickly effective (for folks to
gain insight) to explore with them the impact of remote work thru the
perspective of motivation and advantages of multilearning and
cross-learning, and the connection to adaptiveness

after discussions, they ack'd and grasped that their prior min learning was
now approaching zero, and that the clock is ticking on further reduction of
adaptiveness

to state the obvious: i now suspect that most discussions and research of
"remote working is working" are based on the paradigm of individuals doing
individual tasks (vs real teams swarming & cross-learning), and also
assuming singe-specialization workers (vs multilearning)

aside: predict this is a temp problem and one day great AR tech will make
remoteness work for many contexts, including swarming and real teams
