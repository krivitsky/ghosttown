fw - framework; lm - learning machine

suspect we have a diff background on the concept of CF team; it has always
meant to us, *every* functional skill needed so that a team can do
*everything* to create & deliver a product... e.g., in sw: UX analysis, biz
analysis, HI design, DB design, architectural, coding, ... operations
deployment, etc. and in hw (over the years been involved in LeSS-related
adoptions creating video equip, printers, network elements, car... spanning
motors, chips, PCBs, sensors, etc), from "layout to thermal to suppliers",
etc

years ago in LeSS-1 book we pointed to the classic literature on a real CF
team as spanning engineering, marketing, & manufacturing, so we have always
assumed a wide scope and meant this. to quote from book 1 (p. 197): "*Cross-functional
means that team membership includes all the key functions involved in the
project, usually Engineering, Marketing, and Manufacturing*."

that quote is from the *Teams* chapter in LeSS 1 book (one of the candidate
readings)

of course, in hw-dev large traditional silo'd org, a LeSS adoption doesn't
go from current state to the above quickly. so in LeSS (covered in the
adoption chapter in book 3) there are incremental change techniques for
hw-related dev such as expanding DoD, FTAM, etc.
