as an example, in the bmw autonomous driving (AD) product R&D (where some
of us are involved in a LeSS adoption), there is a unique need for
phd-level research activities. this is true applied scientific research
involving study of recently published leading-edge papers in some highly
technical areas of machine learning, etc, and evaluating promising paths
from these papers requiring phd-level knowledge.

this experiment is *not* about "fake study/research" which is just the
normal activities of UX analysis, design, item refinement, architecture,
etc. this is about true scientific research

the default guide in LeSS related to true research and innovation items is
that a FT volunteers for it, and explores (book 3, pp 209-210, and also in
book 2 *Requirements & PBI* chapter)... versus having a separate "research
group"

but in the AD case, there is no one FT that has a high enough % of phds
with the similar specializations to work through all the papers, and pick a
promising path

what to do?

LeSS Experiment: Research Community

   1. PhD researchers join feature teams, as usual, and participate in
   regular feature development/programming
   2. The PhD researchers (and anyone) form a Research Community (a broad
   one, not many fine-grained ones)
   3. The researchers in the community (or a sub-group of the community
   interested in a particular area) meet to identify papers relevant to the
   current research top to study & then split the study of the papers amongst
   them
   4. They later meet to feedback & discuss
   5. They identify a concrete experiment to try by a feature team, usually
   including software and/or hardware prototyping. At this point, a regular
   feature team is taking over more narrow research, usually including
   prototyping or other experiments.
   6. the regular feature team, with at least one researcher in it that
   knows the paper chosen, explores the "experiment"
   7. feedback & evolve as necessary (e.g., feedback to PO at Review,
   feedback to Research Community)

this could be framed as something like *Research Community + Leading Team +
Take a Bite*, in terms of LeSS guides
