have just decided, with angela johnson and her team, to run my currently
scheduled july 15 CLP in minneapolis

she has her own facility and can design/control all the safety measures.
there will be lots of surface cleaning, non-crowding, masks in at least
common areas, non-touch temperature-reading devices, and more

angela and her team have already been designing & delivering in-house
courses/events that been done more safely, so this "safer" CLP design is
building on their experience

believe her goal is also to create a video in the next few days detailing
the guest experience in the course, from arrival to classroom seating to
exercises to food to the belgian beer ;) will share that if it gets
created

my CLP focuses on teams of "4" working together at giant whiteboard-wall
areas (for systems modeling); to realize this, am taking over the entire
facility and all the rooms, so that each team has their own room or hallway
for exercises, and can distance from each other, while still talking and
sketching/learning together in a spread-out semi-circle around the wall.
people will be writing on big post-its in big size to be able to see the
wall models from some distance.

it now seems that the virus is also far more dangerous for those with prior
co-morbidities (diabetes, ...) that have metabolic dysfunctions and
weakened immune systems. so may recommend people in those categories to not
join

c

PS until there is great AR tech to go virtual (5-15 yrs?), there is
currently no match -- in my observation -- for the quality and impact of
the learning experience of having a small team at a big whiteboard
sketching and having a conversation, for many hours of the course. and this
is core to my CLP. don't personally want my guests to have anything less
than this level of quality/experience
