hi. hinted at in book 3 preface (p. x). in general we are following scrum guide caps style, so when very specifically highlighting a role in scrum/LeSS (for targeted statements about the specific role), we will (usually) capitalize. note also we use *Team* as short for the SG role name *Dev Team*.

*e.g. "Scrum Masters are responsible for a well-working LeSS adoption. Their focus is towards the Teams, Product Owner, organization, and development practices."*
we are trying to highlight the official role


OTOH if we are *not* trying to highlight a precise/official statement about the role of the Team in scrum/LeSS, we will use "team" or "teams".


*e.g. "Structure the organization using real teams as the basic organizational building block"*
in this example we are of course actually referring to *Teams*, but there is no compelling reason for us to highlight it with caps, as we aren't trying to emphasize the scrum role per se.


there are surely places where we are inconsistent. but one can perhaps see our motivation: it would look/read somewhat stilted or pedantic to use "Team" in every sentence in the book where we want to write "team".
