over the years, have occasionally worked with some clients that have a product that is always meaningfully customized for each deployment. e.g., ion trading platform deployed into a particular bank, zodiac in-flight entertainment systems. the customization & deployment work is non-trivial. example customization & integration work includes data connectors, biz rules, branding, and more. in those 2 product/client examples, each may have "20" deployment "projects" going on at a time with 20 of their customers

in both those clients, these are handled as deployment projects, by separate project teams (not product teams)

there are various frictions doing it that way, in relation to the stable product teams. some frictions arise from the wastes of handoff, etc. some frictions arise from deployment-specific features that have to be managed & supported for years. some frictions arise from what are originally deployment-specific features that then find their way into the main stream of the product features. some frictions arise fro sales folks selling customization deployments that make a quick buck, but lead to a long-term headache of from having to maintain so many versions for years.

the time line and activities span early customer engagement to discover needs, all they way to much later deployment

one experiment i've played with in the past is "tour of duty" *core-product* feature teams that rotate in and out of the role of dedicated *project* deployment teams for e.g., a year. there's also the possibility to apply Leading Team guide here, as a secondary purpose of Leading Team (besides the main "break the trail on a complex item" purpose) is specification & integration with 3rd parties... but if there are 20 deployment projects but only 10 core-product feature teams, that doesn't "scale"

if you've been in this kind of org, you know that there are some deep and "eternal" tensions (between product dev and deployment projects) in almost any org design one sets up

*QUES >> what are your observations and/or experiments in such orgs that you've seen?*
