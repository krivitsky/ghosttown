connection to scaling: large-scale sw solutions often involve *workflows*

in finance & insurance one sees this mistake, and recently in pharma was
reminded of it: using sw to 'automate' steps of existing internal workflows
& processes, rather than solving the real customer problem ("claim
payout"), maintaining the status quo but with some automation. ie *workflows
over outcomes*. have even seen 'automation' solutions such as switch from
paper to emailed pdfs that are then printed out ;)

one influence is law-1, since solving the customer outcome might reduce or
eliminate the need for existing operational departments. another is *familiarity
bias*

we suggest the opposite, *outcomes over workflows*

which is a specialization of the LeSS guide '*more outcomes less outputs*',
which ideally influences how items are communicated, and which takes us
waaaay back to tom gilb, evo, and value mgmt

have very limited knowledge of safe, so this could easily be wrong, but
have the impression that a 'value stream' is frequently for a workflow (and
makes another list!). if so, that is 'workflow over outcome', leading to
baking in that operational group & stream

one LeSS Huge implication is to *avoid* organizing RAs around workflows or
'value streams'. yet as always, let's avoid false dichotomy bias
