a key idea in original scrum, *multi-learning*, is *not* T-shaped, but
M-shaped
https://peoplecentre.wordpress.com/2017/06/19/the-m-shaped-employee/

"T shaped" was originally meant to imply deep skill in ONE speciality, and
breadth of experience and ability to collaborate across different areas
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T-shaped_skills

T-shaped is a *trivial* level, just a repeat of single-specialization
promotion, far from the vision of multi-learning in M-shaped workers, and
misses a key idea & practice needed if adaptiveness is the optimizing goal:
a person developing depth of skill in 2+ areas. ie *primary, secondary,
tertiary* skills in a person, who is learning even more in new areas

this M-shaped is also what kent beck meant by "paint drip people"
https://twitter.com/kentbeck/status/761223760091320320

"T shaped" has been *incorrectly* widespread used by various
speakers/authors to mean M-shaped, especially incorrectly used in the
agile/scrum world, but that is not its original meaning, nor does the
visual "T" suggest *multiple* deep skills... in contrast to M-shaped

since (1) the original meaning of T-shaped is not what we want for
multi-learning adaptiveness, (2) the visualization of a T is the wrong
visualization for multi-learning adaptiveness, i encourage folks to ....

(1) not use T-shaped, to avoid increasing the ignorance & incorrect
meaning,
(2a) use the more instructive "M shaped" metaphor, or
(2b) avoid all metaphors and just explain multi-learning in simple
language, with a focus on *why*
