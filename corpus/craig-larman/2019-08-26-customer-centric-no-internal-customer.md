(a point made on another thread; worth highlighting to all candidate trainers...)
am not at all a fan of the phrase or concept "internal customer." afaik, bas feels the same

why? first, am hardly the first to make the point. e.g.
https://www.salesprocessengineering.net/2008/07/07/theres-no-such-thing-as-an-internal-customer/

there are internal stakeholders and users, no doubt. if an organization fools themselves that the internal people -- none of whom are paying $$ to the company -- are customers in the same league as real customers who pay you money, suggest that is the starting point of an org losing its way, and losing real customers. in LeSS, the principle "customer centric" does not refer to internal people; it refers to real (ideally, paying) external customers

focus from their view

in addition to the issues in the referenced link, the "internal customer" framing can easily lead to the delusion that there is "internal value"; e.g.

"our BA group creates the value of a req doc for their customer... the developers."

that might sound funny, but have actually heard this kind of language-game distortions, which happens ala Larman's Laws, etc. or similarly, in value-stream mapping, what is actually waste in the eyes of paying customers is redefined as "value" because "internal customers" have been defined.
