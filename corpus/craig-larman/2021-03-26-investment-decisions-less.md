as "Large-Scale Scrum is Scrum", investment decisions are made by the independent Product Owner; there is no "contract game" where the PO is responsible for doing what someone else directs, or goals to conform to

the question might assume a traditional annual operating budget process with funding of projects/programs/initiatives model; or a traditional big-batch "goals hierarchy" model where higher goals (decided & funded by higher ups) are decomposed to mid goals, and so on down (one often sees this in traditional-mgmt banks & insurance companies). iirc SAFE (which hardly know) has something like that. but all that is eliminated in a LeSS adoption. there is (simply) "1000 developers" with a (simple to calc) annual total salary to fund; then the PO decides what to do adaptively, each Sprint. it is a delegation model in which *all* decision-making authority of what happens -- large or small -- in the product is delegated to the PO

in LeSS Huge, this expands to the PO and APOs; no one else decides what to do. and to reiterate, there are no "conformance to goals" imposed from others

platform teams, shared service teams, and other component teams (these are all component teams), are also eliminated in a LeSS adoption; it is based on a shared code (collective code ownership) model of feature teams that do all the work e2e. these teams are also usually responsible for all "operations"-related work

it's a dramatic simplification or descaling not only of the product group, but also of the financing and decision-making structures & policies

one of the big ideas in Scrum (and hence LeSS) is that it is a *meta-framework for empirical control*, and thus, *on purpose*, is *silent* on specific practices (in dev, in prod mgmt, etc). it isn't that ken (or me and bas) don't know myriad prescriptive frameworks and practices or forgot to add them. rather, these are all left out on purpose, to reduce prescriptiveness and increase adaptiveness/learning so that the group adopts and installs whatever they want to experiment with, including ideas you mention above. and which they'll also change over time.
