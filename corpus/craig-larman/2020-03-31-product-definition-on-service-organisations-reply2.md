rowan,

thx for sharing these thoughts. from a systems thinking view, one might say
in general, "of course, start with redesigning and improving the deeper
problem X, before the sw product."

would guess most everyone here is mindful of that goal

for example, often have done gigantic-walls-based value-stream mapping
workshops with people in an org, and then we might discover (and visualize)
that most of the wastes (delay, handoff, ...) happen LONG before a feature
request gets to the R&D group. and in these myriad cases most of the
improvement would come from improving the Sales or Marketing or etc,
without even touching the 1K group of R&D people

one lands at similar insights from the Evo & "Value Planning" methods of
Gilb, Seddon, Impact/Effect mgmt, etc. (aside: Seddon is mentioned in larman's
law #5 elaboration
<https://www.craiglarman.com/wiki/index.php?title=Larman%27s_Laws_of_Organizational_Behavior>
)

*but here comes a but*: e.g. (1) the consultant (e.g. me, you) is brought
in by senior R&D mgmt to help with a deep org change to LeSS for their
hw/sw product group. then (2) we do VSM and discover that most of the
problems are upstream or in other parts of the org system. then (3) one
discovers that there is no R&D senior mgr within our sphere that has more
than very marginal influence in those other areas (Sales, ...), and the
people in those other areas have no idea about LeSS or improving, and no
interest in change.

in these kind of cases, which at least for me have been regular, one has to
consider the political and change reality of where we can move the needle
to get some change traction and win hearts and minds. so then we may be
limited to a "local optimization" in R&D and Product Mgmt (and the related
influences in HR and Finance), knowing that this is not solving the deeper
problems. also -- a point that bas used to make regularly -- it can be
difficult to sell a message of deep change into other areas (a message
emerging from the R&D group) when the R&D group still... sucks. so in that
case the art of the possible may be to try to make R&D & Product Mgmt (the
key product group parties in a LeSS adoption) much better (adaptiveness,
...) and then use that as a motivating existence proof to change wider

related to above: LeSS was created with a focus on hw/sw product groups,
and that is its primary fit. likewise with the experience and knowledge of
the trainers and coaches. if that's the case, showing success in one's area
of expertise for change (e.g. a product group), before potentially
blundering into deep and unfamiliar waters, can again help win hearts and
minds.

if one is brought in at the beginning by the Board and/or CEO with the
remit, "redesign the entire company to fix the major problems", then
starting with truly systemic analysis and change is of course on the table.
but if one is brought in for change in a hw/sw product group, then
politically or in terms of the realistic change dynamics, one may have to
start locally within the product group, before widening

this is not a recommendation per se to start primarily within a product
group, but an explanation of forces which have led us and others to start
there
