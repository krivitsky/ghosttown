## Notes on Evolution

**Sharpening over time:** The AI-related stances (biases 3, 4, 12, 16, 17) are the clearest evolution in the corpus. Early 2026 newsletters show genuine ambivalence (TBM 405: "I desperately hope the optimistic future awaits"). By mid-2026 (TBM 425, 426), the critique of how AI mandates are being deployed has intensified significantly. The AI skepticism is not about the technology but about the organizational and political dynamics surrounding its deployment.

**Consistent through entire corpus:** Skepticism of rollups/metrics proxies (biases 1, 2), advocacy for WIP reduction and outcome-centricity (biases 8, 9), and the legibility/mētis tension (bias 6) appear in the earliest and latest materials with no meaningful softening.

**Evolving:** The "mirrors to mirroring" shift (bias 19) represents a genuine change of view — Cutler acknowledges earlier in TBM 426 that he "used to be smitten" by transparency as a fix. This is a live evolution, not a settled position.

**Legitimacy concern is new:** The explicit political economy critique of AI-as-power-consolidation (bias 16) is new as of ~2026 and shows the most anxiety — he names names (Dorsey, Curtis Yarvin, accelerationists) and describes these as "mainstream in tech," not fringe.
