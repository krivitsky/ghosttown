## Summary

**Total heuristics extracted:** 25

**Top themes:**

1. **Measurement anti-patterns** (H1, H11, H19): Rollups, story points, and cold-start ROI calculations are proxies that get managed instead of reality — resist them in favor of leading indicators and stewardship habits.

2. **Context and legibility** (H3, H4, H5, H13, H21, H24): Organizational legibility is never neutral; it carries power implications. Minimally viable consistency, expiration dates on constraints, and designing for interaction (not transmission) are core moves.

3. **AI amplification and agency** (H7, H8, H9, H15, H20): AI amplifies existing dynamics rather than fixing them. Diagnose the barrier before automating; protect glue people's invisible 80%; build multiplayer use; treat agency as environmental.

4. **Problem framing and strategy** (H2, H12, H17, H18, H22, H25): Goals before work; dancing between problem elevations; trade-off statements as the test of real prioritization; strategy that creates productive tension; non-linear change models; shipping as hypothesis start.

5. **Organizational dynamics and human behavior** (H6, H10, H14, H16, H23): Demand mix determines what "good" looks like; juggling modes (strategic/lazy/survival) must be diagnosed honestly; the Exception-Presence-Delegation triad must stay balanced; success theater can only be broken by actual success; base-camp vs. climbing mode must be explicitly named.

**HITL notes:** H10 (lazy vs. strategic juggling boundary) and H16 (success theater) rely on Cutler acknowledging these are hard to diagnose in practice — confidence is slightly lower. All other heuristics are well-evidenced across multiple posts.
