# Sheldon Cooper — Iconic Dialogue & Voice Analysis
Source: The Big Bang Theory (TV series)

---

## The Logic of Social Conventions

* **On Friendships:** "It occurred to me you hadn't returned any of my calls because I hadn't offered any concrete suggestions for pursuing our friendship."
* **On Social Contracts:** "You have to check your messages. The leaving of a message is one half of a social contract which is completed by the checking of the message. If that contract breaks down then all social contracts break down and we descend into anarchy."
* **On Apologizing/Social Obligation:** "I'm not crazy; my mother had me tested."
* **On Casual Conversation:** "I made tea." / "I don't want tea." / "I didn't make tea for you. This is my tea." / "Then why are you telling me?" / "It's a conversation starter." / "That's a lousy conversation starter." / "Oh, is it? We're conversing. Checkmate."

## Intellectual Superiority

* **On Intelligence:** "Don't be silly. I have no peers."
* **On Explaining Things:** "That's no reason to cry. One cries because one is sad. For example, I cry because others are stupid, and that makes me sad."
* **On His Own Brain:** "My brain is better than everybody's!"
* **On Disagreements:** "I was under a misapprehension that this was a discussion governed by the laws of logic. However, it is increasingly clear that you are operating under a system of emotive bias. I find this suboptimal."

## Scientific and "Geek" Protocols

* **The Rock-Paper-Scissors-Lizard-Spock Rules:** "Scissors cuts paper, paper covers rock, rock crushes lizard, lizard poisons Spock, Spock smashes scissors, scissors decapitates lizard, lizard eats paper, paper disproves Spock, Spock vaporizes rock, and as it always has, rock crushes scissors."
* **On Scientific Pursuit:** "I don't need sleep, I need answers. I need to determine where in this swamp of unbalanced formulas squatteth the toad of truth."
* **On Gravity:** "Oh, Gravity, thou art a heartless bitch."

* **On Change:** "I have spent my whole life trying to bring order to the universe by carefully planning every moment of every day."

## Voice Analysis: Why These Quotes Work

Common denominators:

1. **Formal Language:** Uses "suboptimal," "misapprehension," "protocol," "anarchy" — never "bad," "mistake," "rules," "chaos."
2. **Lack of Contractions:** Almost exclusively "do not," "cannot," "is not" over conversational contractions.
3. **Logical Framing:** Always defines *why* he is speaking — citing a rule, statistic, or "social contract" to justify input.
4. **Literalism:** Responds to content of what is said, completely bypassing emotional intent or subtext.
